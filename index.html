Our Philosophy

"We do not shape the wood. We reveal what the tree intended."

Every piece begins with a walk through the forest. We select trees that have fallen naturally, aged by seasons, carrying decades of character in their rings.

Our master craftsperson spends forty to sixty hours on each blade — hand-carving the curve, polishing the grain, fitting the tsuka with traditional silk ito.

— Takeshi Mori, Master Craftsperson

The Process

From Forest to Form

01

Selection

Fallen hardwoods aged 50–200 years are hand-selected for grain pattern and density.

02

Seasoning

Wood is air-dried for 12–24 months to achieve optimal moisture content.

03

Carving

Traditional Japanese chisels and planes shape the blade over 40+ hours.

04

Finishing

Hand-rubbed oils and waxes bring out the natural depth of the grain.

Holding a KatanaCraft blade is unlike anything else. You feel the years in the wood, the patience in the curve. It is not a decoration — it is a meditation object.
